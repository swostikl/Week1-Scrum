## Task1 : Reverse Engineering - User Stories


### User Registration and Login
- *As a user, I want to create an account, so my data is saved securely.
- *As a user, I want to log in and reset my password if I forget it.


### Browsing and Searching for Products
- *As a customer, I need to search for products using keywords, categories, or filters, so that I can quickly find the items I am looking for.*
- *As a user, I need to browse products by categories, so that I can discover items I am interested in.*



### Product Details and Recommendations 
- *As a customer, I need to view detailed product information including images, price, and reviews, so that I can make informed purchase decisions.*
- *As a user, I need to receive personalized product recommendations based on my browsing and purchase history, so that I can discover relevant products.*

### Payment and Order Management
- *As a buyer, I need multiple payment options such as credit card, debit card, and gift cards, so that I can pay using my preferred method.*
- *As a customer, I need to track my orders and view delivery status, so that I know when my purchases will arrive.*

### Reviews and Feedback
- *As a customer, I need to leave product reviews and ratings, so that I can share my experience with other shoppers.*
- *As a user, I need to read product reviews and ratings from other customers, so that I can make better purchasing decisions.*

## Task 2: Building the App

**1. Product Owner (PO)**
  - Defines product vision
  - Manages product backlog and prioritizes user stories
  - Collaborates with stakeholders and development team
  - Ensures that the team is always working on the most valuable and important tasks first.

- **Role of the Product owner within scrum**
    - In Scrum, the Product Owner (PO) is responsible for maximizing the value of the product created by the Scrum Team.
    - Manages the product backlog and prioritize features
    - Ensure backlog items are clearly described, ordered by importance and value, and understood by the development team.
    - Define user stories and clarify requirements throughout the development process.

**2. Scrum Master**

Having a **Scrum Master** is essential for maintaining a smooth flow of communication and ensuring that the Scrum framework is followed correctly.
  - Facilitates Scrum ceremonies(Sprint Planning, Daily Stand-up, Sprint Review) ensuring the Scrum framework is followed correctly.
  - Removes obstacles that may hinder the development of teams's progress.
Without Scum master the team might lose on Scrum principle, leading to unclear process, poor communication and reduced productivity. so it is good tom have. Therefor having a Scrum Master is recommende for workflow and successful project delivery.

**5. Burndown Chart in the context of Scrum**

A burndown chart is a graphical representation of remaining work versus time in a sprint. It helps the team monitor progress, predict completion, and identifies obstacles or bottlenecks early.

---

**6. The three Key Scrum Ceremonies**
1. **Sprint Planning** – Select backlog items for the sprint and break them into tasks and assigned tasks to the team members. 
2. **Daily Scrum (Standup)** – 15-minute daily meeting to discuss progress and blockers  
3. **Sprint Review** – Demonstrate completed work and gather feedback  for future improvements. 

---

**7. Difference between product backlog and sprint backlog**
- **Product Backlog:** Complete list of features, enhancements, and bug fixes. Prioritized by Product Owner.  
- **Sprint Backlog:** Subset of the product backlog selected for a sprint, broken down into actionable tasks and technical details.
